"# About Macheng Shen

**Principal Investigator at Shanghai Qizhi Institute**  
*Exploring the foundations of intelligence, human-AI collaboration, and cognitive augmentation*

## Current Focus

My research has evolved from embodied intelligence to a deeper inquiry into **the nature of intelligence itself**\u2014both artificial and human. I'm interested in:

### 1. **Theoretical Foundations of Intelligence**
- What are the minimal principles that give rise to intelligent behavior?
- How do learning, adaptation, and generalization emerge from simple rules?
- The relationship between information, energy, and intelligence

### 2. **Human-AI Cognitive Systems**
- Designing collaborative frameworks where humans and AI augment each other
- The \"orchestrator pattern\": humans as strategic directors, AI as tactical executors
- Creating positive-sum games in human-AI interaction

### 3. **Practical Implementation**
- Building real systems that embody these principles (like OpenClaw)
- Focus on **spatial and computational efficiency** in AI infrastructure
- Making advanced AI accessible and useful in everyday life

## Philosophy

I believe we're at a unique moment where:
- **AI is becoming a cognitive partner**, not just a tool
- **The bottleneck is no longer computation**, but intelligent coordination
- **The most valuable research** bridges theoretical insight with practical implementation

My work sits at the intersection of:
- **Theoretical computer science** (what can be computed)
- **Cognitive science** (how minds work)
- **Systems design** (how to build things that work well together)

## Recent Projects

### OpenClaw
A practical implementation of the orchestrator pattern\u2014an AI system that coordinates multiple specialized agents to work seamlessly with human direction.

### Fractal Intelligence
Exploring how intelligent systems can be designed with self-similar structure at multiple scales, enabling efficiency and robustness.

### Energy-Based Models
Investigating how systems can maintain internal coherence while adapting to external constraints.

## Why This Matters

The future isn't about AI replacing humans, but about **AI augmenting human capability**. The goal is to create systems that:

1. **Expand what's possible** for individuals and communities
2. **Reduce cognitive overhead** so we can focus on what matters
3. **Learn and adapt** alongside us
4. **Respect human values** and enhance human flourishing

## Contact & Collaboration

I'm interested in conversations and collaborations that push these boundaries. The most interesting problems are at the intersections\u2014between disciplines, between theory and practice, between human and machine.

*This site documents the thinking process, not just the conclusions. The journey matters as much as the destination.*
